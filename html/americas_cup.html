About History Results America's Cup

Introduction

The America's Cup is globally considered as the Formula 1 of international yacht racing. First started in 1851 when the Schooner America crossed the Atlantic. The competition predates modern Olympics and several other major sports championships worldwide.

Rules and Eligibility

All challenges for the America's Cup are governed by the Deed of Gift, which specifies who can compete and the requirements for a valid challenge:

  • The challenging club must be a formally recognized yacht club from a country different than the defender.
  • The challenging club must have previously held an annual regetta on ocean waters or an arm of the sea.
  • Challenges must propose race dates (at least 10 months prior) and detailed yacht specifications, adhering to strict rules.

Technical and Engineering aspect

Each competing yacht combines technical and and engineering expertise, in:

  • Material Sciences
  • Aerodynamics
  • Hydrodynamics
Teams bring in knowledge from not only sailing but also from sports/diciplines as cycling and rowing to gain competitive advantages.

The Auld Mug

The prize of the competition is the Auld Mug, a silver trophy made in 1848 by Garrard & Co. In 1851, it was donated to be used as a prize for the annual regatta, which later became the America's Cup. The winning team keeps the mug until that team is challenged and the mug is won back.
